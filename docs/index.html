The problem: LLMs draft treatment proposals fluently but have no way to verify which jurisdiction requirements are real, which clinician licenses are current, or whether a proposed treatment contradicts a patient's own known conditions — letting one administer treatment directly invites liability.

The solution: An independent Clinical Practice Governor seals the LLM and enforces four hard gates: treatment proposals must cite real jurisdiction requirements (spec-basis), have complete licensing proof (evidence), avoid patient contraindications (set-membership, not inference), and confirm the clinician's license is current. No treatment administers without human sign-off. Every decision lands on an immutable audit ledger.

What it is not: This blueprint does not hold a practice license in any jurisdiction — the operator (licensed physician/dentist) does. It does not model full drug-interaction databases or replace EHR integrations. It supplies the governed, spec-cited, audited execution scaffold so your practice doesn't rebuild the compliance layer from scratch.
